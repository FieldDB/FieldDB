Frequently Asked Questions


Tips:
  • First time using? Try our tutorial to get familiar with the app.
  • To return to the main page at any time, click on the LingSync logo at the top left of the page.
  • Save your work often! Changes are automatically saved every five minutes, but it is good practice to save frequently.
  • Use your browser's reload button to discard changes.
  • If a change in your settings isn’t showing up immediately, save your data and click the sync button (↻); this should update everything, and will also keep your auto-parser performing at its best.
How do I create an account?
From the main startup screen, click the "Register" button in the top right corner and fill out the form. If you are unsure as to which server you should select, you most likely want to choose "LingSync".
How do I create a corpus?
After you have logged in, hover over "Settings" and select "Corpus". From this page, you can add a new corpus.
How do I enter data?
After you have selected a corpus and an elicitation session (or created a new session), you will be taken to the data entry page. The topmost item on this page is the space to enter new records. Fill in your data and/or record/upload audio and then click "Create New". Note that your new record will not be saved to the database until you click the "Save" button at the top right of the screen!
How do I import data?
You can import entire Elicitation Session by uploading an audio/video (any format works: .wav, .aiff, .mp3, .ogg, .mov, .avi, .3gp, .mp4 etc) (how-to video). or text data (csv, plain text, TextGrid, toolbox and more) (how-to video) using the Prototype app. These import functionality will be moved to the corpus admin pages when it becomes a priority.
How can I reduce my audio/video upload size?
If you have heavy video files (more than 300MB) you should consider using a private or unlisted video on YouTube, SoundCloud, Google drive or even your department's servers to host the file online. You can still import the audio/video into LingSync by extracting just the audio track and uploading it. FFmpeg makes it easy to convert any audio/video to mp3. Download FFmpeg and put it in the same directory as your video (or install it on your system if you know how). FFmpeg is very very useful for linguists so its a great tool to have on your computer. After you have it, then open a terminal/console and type something like this: 
cd folderwheremyvideofilesare
ffmpeg -i myoriginalvideofilename.mp4 myoriginalvideofilename.mp3
(where mp4 is the original file type)
How do I edit an existing record?
Single-click anywhere on the record you wish to edit. This will make the record editable. Make your changes and click the "Done" button. Note that changes to this record will not be saved to the database until you click the "Save" button!
How do I record audio, upload audio, or add/view comments?
Click the expand button on a new record or a record in edit mode to access audio and comment features. You may click the collapse button to hide this section.

Recording Audio
To record audio, click the record button. Note that you must then click the "Allow" button on the browser dropdown at the top of the page. Your audio will begin recording once you click "Allow". To stop recording, simply click the "Recording" button. Your audio will then be processed. This may take a while.

Uploading Audio
To upload audio files, click on "Choose files" and select one or more audio files from your hard drive. Audio files may be in .wav, .mp3, or .ogg format.

Adding/Deleting Comments
To add a comment, click on the "Add a comment" link and enter your comment in the popup box. To delete a comment, click on the "X" next to the comment. You may only delete your own comments. Comments are not editable, so to change what your comment says simply delete it and write a new one.
How do I delete a record or audio file?
To delete a record, click the relevant record and then click the "Delete" button. To delete an audio file, click on the "X" next to the file. In both cases, you will be prompted to verify the deletion. Note that deleting records and files only a flag and does not result in destroying data.
Is my data private and protected?
Yes! For more on our policies with respect to your data, please see our web site.
How do I export data?
You can export your entire database as csv (warning, csv is a lossy format, don't use this for backing up your data), or backup your data as non-lossy json. You can also share your data with other people by adding them to your corpus, or you can make parts of your corpus public and sharable on your corpus' own webpage You can also export lists of data in LaTeX among other things using the Prototype app.

You can also download a word list, glossed word list and a morphemes list, among other things. If you're curious about other exports you can do, contact us and one of the current students will give you a tutorial. Exporting data from LingSync.org is extremely powerful and flexible.
